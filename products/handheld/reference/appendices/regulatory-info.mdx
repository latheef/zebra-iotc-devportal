# Regulatory & Regional Information

📕 **REFERENCE**: Region codes, frequency bands, supported countries, and compliance certifications for the RFD40/RFD90 reader sleds.

## Overview

UHF RFID operates in the 860–960 MHz Industrial, Scientific, and Medical (ISM) band. The exact frequencies, power limits, and channel plans vary by country and regulatory authority. The device must be configured with the correct regulatory region before RFID operations can begin.

> **Warning:** Operating an RFID reader in a region for which it is not certified is a violation of radio frequency regulations and may result in interference with licensed services, legal penalties, or both. Always configure the correct region.

## Setting the Regulatory Region

The regulatory region is set using the **123RFID Desktop** application. It cannot be changed remotely via MQTT. This restriction exists to prevent accidental or unauthorized changes to regulatory-controlled radio parameters.

Once set, the region code is stored persistently on the device and survives reboots and factory resets of application settings.

## Supported Countries by Region Code

### NA: North America (FCC Part 15.247)

United States, Canada, Mexico, and other countries that follow FCC Part 15 regulations.

### EU: Europe (ETSI EN 302 208)

All EU member states, United Kingdom, Norway, Switzerland, Iceland, Turkey, and other countries that follow ETSI regulations.

### JP: Japan (ARIB STD-T106)

Japan. Uses Listen Before Talk (LBT) protocol, where the reader checks for channel occupancy before transmitting.

### CN: China (SRRC)

People's Republic of China. Requires separate SRRC type approval.

## Power Considerations by Region

When configuring transmit power via the `transmitPowerIndex` setting, be aware that the maximum effective power varies by region:

| Region | Max Index | Approximate Max Power |
|---|---|---|
| NA (FCC) | 300 | 30.0 dBm EIRP |
| EU (ETSI) | 270 | 27.0 dBm ERP (≈ 29.15 dBm EIRP) |
| JP (ARIB) | 300 | 30.0 dBm EIRP |
| Others | Varies | Check region-specific documentation |

The device firmware enforces the regulatory power limit for the configured region. Setting a `transmitPowerIndex` above the regional maximum results in the power being capped at the regulatory limit, no error is returned, but the actual output power does not exceed the legal maximum.

> **Tip:** For maximum interoperability across regions, design your application to query the device configuration and respect the regional power limits rather than hardcoding a specific transmit power value.
