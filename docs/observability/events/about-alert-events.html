About Alert Events

Alert events fire when a monitored device metric crosses a configured threshold. They are low-frequency, high-importance events designed to trigger operational responses, replacing a low battery, investigating overheating, or addressing connectivity issues.

Alert Types​

The RFD40/RFD90 generates alerts for the following conditions:

Battery Low (BATTERY_LOW)​

Fires when the battery level drops below the warning threshold (default: 20%). Severity: WARNING.

This alert signals that the device will need charging or a battery swap soon. In a warehouse shift context, this typically means the device has 1–2 hours of active use remaining.

Battery Critical (BATTERY_CRITICAL)​

Fires when the battery level drops below the critical threshold (default: 10%). Severity: CRITICAL.

At this level, the device may power off unexpectedly during an RFID operation. Immediate action is required; dock the device or swap the battery.

Temperature High (TEMPERATURE_HIGH)​

Fires when the internal device temperature exceeds the configured threshold (default: 45°C). Severity: WARNING or CRITICAL depending on the temperature.

Sustained high temperatures can indicate:

  • Extended high-power RF scanning sessions
  • Device in direct sunlight or near heat sources
  • Hardware malfunction

Connection Degraded (CONNECTION_DEGRADED)​

Fires when the Wi-Fi signal strength or Bluetooth link quality falls below acceptable levels. Severity: WARNING.

Degraded connectivity may cause:

  • Delayed dataEVT delivery
  • Missed MQTT messages (especially at QoS 0)
  • Eventual disconnection if conditions persist

Operation Failed (OPERATION_FAILED)​

Fires when an RFID operation terminates unexpectedly due to an error. Severity: ERROR.

This may indicate RF hardware issues, antenna faults, or firmware errors that prevented the operation from completing normally.

alerts vs. alert_short​

The reader can publish alerts in two formats simultaneously:

FormatPayload SizeContentUse Case
alertsFull (~300–500 bytes)Complete context: device state snapshot, threshold, messageRich monitoring systems
alert_shortCompact (~80–120 bytes)Essential fields only: type, severity, valueBandwidth-constrained or high-volume fleets

Both formats carry the same alert_type and severity. The difference is the amount of contextual data included. You can enable one or both via config_events.

De-bounce Behavior​

To prevent alert storms when a metric oscillates around a threshold, the reader firmware implements de-bounce logic:

  1. Initial trigger: When the metric first crosses the threshold, the alert fires immediately
  2. Suppression window: After firing, the same alert type is suppressed for a configurable period (typically 60 seconds)
  3. Re-trigger: If the metric is still in the alert condition after the suppression window, the alert fires again
  4. Clear condition: When the metric returns to a normal value and stays there through a full suppression window, the alert is eligible to re-trigger on the next breach

This prevents scenarios like a battery bouncing between 19% and 21% from generating hundreds of BATTERY_LOW alerts per minute.

Example Timeline​

10:00:00 - Battery at 21% → no alert
10:00:30 - Battery at 19% → BATTERY_LOW alert fires
10:00:45 - Battery at 20% → suppressed (within window)
10:01:00 - Battery at 18% → suppressed (within window)
10:01:30 - Battery at 17% → BATTERY_LOW alert fires again (window expired)

Severity Levels​

SeverityMeaningRecommended Action
WARNINGApproaching a limit; action recommendedSchedule maintenance, notify supervisor
CRITICALAt or beyond a dangerous limit; immediate action requiredStop operations, swap device
ERROROperation failure; system is impactedInvestigate, restart operation or device

Subscribing to Alerts​

Subscribe to alerts for a specific device:

{tenantId}/DATA/clients/alerts/{deviceSerial}

Subscribe to alerts for all devices in the fleet:

{tenantId}/DATA/clients/alerts/+

Note: Alerts are published at QoS 1, ensuring at-least-once delivery. Your application should handle potential duplicate alert deliveries by deduplicating on device_serial + timestamp + alert_type.
