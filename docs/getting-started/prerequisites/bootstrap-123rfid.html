Reader Bootstrap with 123RFID Desktop

📗 TUTORIAL: This tutorial walks you through the one-time bootstrap process for your RFD40 or RFD90 reader sled using the 123RFID Desktop application. By the end, your reader will be configured to connect to the Zebra IoT Connector platform over Wi-Fi.

Prerequisites​

  • RFD40 or RFD90 reader sled
  • Windows 10 or Windows 11 PC with 123RFID Desktop installed
  • USB cable (USB-A to USB-C or USB-C to USB-C)
  • Wi-Fi network SSID and password
  • IOTC tenant credentials (see How to Obtain IOTC Credentials)

Step 1: Connect the Reader via USB​

  1. Power on the reader sled by pressing and holding the power button for 3 seconds.
  2. Connect the reader to your Windows PC using the USB cable.
  3. Wait for the reader to appear as a connected device (the LED indicator should turn solid blue).

Note: If Windows prompts you to install drivers, allow the installation to complete before proceeding.

Step 2: Launch 123RFID Desktop​

  1. Open 123RFID Desktop on your Windows PC.
  2. The application automatically scans for connected readers.
  3. Your reader should appear in the Connected Readers panel with its serial number and model (RFD40 or RFD90).
  4. Click on the reader entry to select it.

Step 3: Set the Regulatory Region​

  1. Navigate to Configuration → Region Settings.
  2. Select the appropriate regulatory region for your operating location (e.g., NA for North America, EU for Europe, JP for Japan).
  3. Click Apply.

Warning: The regulatory region can only be configured through 123RFID Desktop via USB connection. This setting cannot be changed remotely via MQTT. Operating a reader in the wrong region may violate local radio frequency regulations.

Step 4: Configure Wi-Fi​

  1. Navigate to Configuration → Wi-Fi Settings.
  2. Enter the following:
    • SSID: Your Wi-Fi network name
    • Security Type: WPA2-PSK (or as appropriate for your network)
    • Password: Your Wi-Fi network password
  3. Click Apply.
  4. The reader will attempt to connect to the specified Wi-Fi network. A successful connection is indicated by the Wi-Fi LED turning solid green.

Step 5: Configure the MDM Endpoint​

  1. Navigate to Configuration → MDM / Cloud Settings.
  2. Enter the following IOTC connection details:
    • Broker Host: Your IOTC MQTT broker hostname (e.g., mqtt.iotconnector.zebra.com)
    • Broker Port: 8883
    • Tenant ID: Your IOTC tenant identifier
    • Username: Your MQTT username
    • Password: Your MQTT password
  3. Click Apply.

The reader will establish an MQTT connection to the IOTC broker. A successful connection is indicated in the status panel.

Step 6: Verify the Connection​

  1. In 123RFID Desktop, navigate to Status → Cloud Connection.
  2. Confirm the status shows Connected.
  3. Note the Device Serial Number displayed; you will need this for MQTT topic construction.

Step 7: Disconnect USB​

  1. Once the connection is verified, safely disconnect the USB cable.
  2. The reader will continue operating on battery power and maintain its Wi-Fi and MQTT connections.

The bootstrap process is complete. Your reader is now registered with the IOTC platform and communicating via MQTT.

Configuration Summary​

After completing bootstrap, your reader has the following configuration:

SettingValue
RegionSet to your regulatory region
Wi-FiConnected to your specified network
MQTT BrokerConnected to IOTC platform
ProtocolMQTT 3.1.1 over TLS (port 8883)

Troubleshooting​

IssueResolution
Reader not detected via USBTry a different USB cable or port. Ensure reader is powered on.
Wi-Fi connection failsVerify SSID and password. Ensure the network is within range. Check that the network supports 2.4 GHz.
MDM connection failsVerify broker hostname, port, username, and password. Check that port 8883 is not blocked by your network firewall.
