How to Obtain IOTC Credentials & Tenant ID

📙 HOW-TO GUIDE: Follow these steps to register for the Zebra IoT Connector platform, create a tenant, and obtain the credentials required for MQTT broker connectivity.

Prerequisites​

  • A valid business email address
  • Access to the Zebra developer portal

Step 1: Register on the Zebra Developer Portal​

  1. Navigate to the Zebra Developer Portal.
  2. Click Sign Up or Create Account.
  3. Fill in your name, business email, company name, and set a password.
  4. Accept the Terms of Service and Developer Agreement.
  5. Check your email for a verification link and confirm your account.

Step 2: Access the IoT Connector Console​

  1. Log in to the Zebra Developer Portal with your verified account.
  2. Navigate to Products → IoT Connector (or search for "IoT Connector" in the portal).
  3. Click Get Started or Request Access to enable IOTC for your account.

Note: Access approval may take up to 24 hours depending on your organization's agreement with Zebra. Contact your Zebra account representative if you need expedited access.

Step 3: Create an IOTC Tenant​

  1. In the IoT Connector console, click Create Tenant.
  2. Provide a Tenant Name; use a descriptive name for your organization or project (e.g., acme-warehouse-west).
  3. Select your Region based on your deployment geography.
  4. Click Create.

Once created, the console displays your Tenant ID; a unique identifier used in all MQTT topic paths.

Step 4: Retrieve Your MQTT Credentials​

After tenant creation, navigate to the Credentials or Connection Details section of your tenant dashboard. You will find:

CredentialExample FormatDescription
Tenant IDabc123def456Unique tenant identifier, used as the first segment in MQTT topics
Broker Hostmqtt.iotconnector.zebra.comMQTT broker endpoint
Broker Port8883TLS-encrypted MQTT port
Usernametenant-user@abc123def456MQTT authentication username
Password(generated)MQTT authentication password

Warning: Store your MQTT password securely. It is shown only once at creation time. If lost, you must regenerate credentials from the tenant dashboard.

Step 5: Record Your Values​

Copy and save the following values; you will need them for reader bootstrap and MQTT client configuration:

Tenant ID:       ___________________________
Broker Host:     ___________________________
Broker Port:     8883
MQTT Username:   ___________________________
MQTT Password:   ___________________________
Device Serial:   ___________________________

The Device Serial is the serial number printed on the label of your RFD40 or RFD90 reader sled. You can also retrieve it via 123RFID Desktop.

Understanding the Topic Structure​

Your Tenant ID forms the root of every MQTT topic used by IOTC:

{tenantId}/{interface}/clients/{channel}/{deviceSerial}

For example, to send a management command to your reader:

abc123def456/MGMT/clients/cmnd/RFD40-1234567890

The four interfaces are:

  • MGMT: Management operations (firmware, configuration, diagnostics)
  • CTRL: Control operations (start/stop inventory, operating modes)
  • DATA: Tag data streams (inventory reads, events)
  • MDM: Mobile Device Management (provisioning, enrollment)

Next Steps​

With your credentials in hand, proceed to Reader Bootstrap with 123RFID Desktop to configure your reader for IOTC connectivity.
